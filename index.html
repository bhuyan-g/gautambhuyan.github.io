Bio

I am currently pursuing a Ph.D. in Astronomy & Astrophysics at Cotton University, specializing in cosmology and dark energy research. My thesis, under the guidance of Dr. Sukanta Deb, focuses on probing dark energy using cosmic tracers. My academic journey includes a B.Sc. and M.Sc. in Physics from Cotton University, where I conducted research on astrometric analysis of open clusters. I have contributed to research projects at leading institutes, including a summer research fellowship at Harish-Chandra Research Institute, where I worked on quantum speed limits. Additionally, I participated in the 7th Indo-French Astronomy School at IUCAA, Pune, where I explored the escape fraction of Lyman continuum leakers. My research has been published in peer-reviewed journals such as the Monthly Notices of the Royal Astronomical Society. I have presented my work at national and international conferences, including the Astronomical Society of India. I am skilled in Python, IDL, Bash, and Fortran, with experience in using tools like lognormal_galaxies, pCIGALE, and SNooPy/SALT2. I also work with various astronomical databases such as OGLE, Gaia, and the Carnegie Supernova Project. Beyond research, I enjoy mentoring students and have guided M.Sc. thesis projects. I am passionate about problem-solving, teamwork, and effective communication in both academic and collaborative settings.
