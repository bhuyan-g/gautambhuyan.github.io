Bio

I’m a Ph.D. candidate in Astronomy & Astrophysics at Cotton University (Guwahati, India), working on precision cosmology using variable stars, extragalactic distance indicators, and large-scale structure. I build reproducible, data-driven pipelines to extract signal from noisy time-series and catalog data, with applications ranging from cosmic distance scales to galaxy–gravitational-wave correlations.

I am a first-author researcher with peer-reviewed work including “Geometry of the LMC based on multiphase analysis of multiwavelength Cepheid light curves using OGLE-IV and Gaia DR3 data” (MNRAS, 2024; DOI: 10.1093/mnras/stad3772, arXiv: 2312.02657).

  • Data science/ML strengths: Python-based analysis (NumPy/Pandas/SciPy), feature extraction from time-series, statistical modeling, and visualization.
  • Statistical methods: Monte Carlo simulation, Bayesian inference (MCMC/emcee), hypothesis testing and model diagnostics.
  • Scale & data sources: Large astronomical datasets (OGLE, Gaia, Carnegie Supernova Project) and catalog cross-matching (CDS/VizieR).
  • Research tooling: Correlation-function workflows (TreeCorr, cKDTree) and simulation tools (e.g., lognormal_galaxies, GWFish).
  • Collaboration: IUCAA Indo–French Astronomy School and HRI summer research; mentoring and supervising M.Sc. thesis projects.
